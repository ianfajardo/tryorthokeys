SA and DSA keycaps are two of the most popular profiles for ortholinear keyboards like the Planck and Preonic. DSA is uniform and low-profile; SA is sculpted and tall. Both look great on an ortho board and give you plenty of options for color and legend style. Here are five sets we recommend for your Planck or Preonic.

</a>

The [Drop Acute keycaps](https://amzn.to/3DlHx8i) are the classic ortholinear keycap set designed by Jack Humbert—the same person behind the Planck, Preonic, and QMK. They feature slim legends and a clean, angled look. Dye-subbed PBT means the legends won’t wear off, and they’re available in dedicated Planck and Preonic kits so you get exactly what you need. If you want the “official” ortho look, Acute is it.

4. **YMDK DSA / Uniform Profile Keycap Set**

<a href="https://amzn.to/3eABKko">

[DSA and XDA](https://amzn.to/3eABKko) are uniform profiles—every row has the same height and shape—which makes them a natural fit for ortholinear layouts. Affordable PBT sets like these often include 100+ keys, so you can cover a Planck or Preonic and have extras for another board. Great for a clean, minimal look and a consistent feel. Blanks are ideal if you’re a touch typist.

5. **DSA Canvas or Neutral DSA-Style Sets**

DSA and DSA-style sets in neutral colors (beige, gray, white) match almost any desk setup. Flat profile means less height and a different sound than SA, and many budget-friendly PBT sets on [Amazon](https://amzn.to/3DlHx8i) and Drop work perfectly for Planck and Preonic. The Drop Acute set above is one option; look for “ortholinear” or “40%” in any listing to make sure you get enough 1u keys.

## Conclusion

Whether you prefer the classic Acute look, the carbon vibe, or the sculpted feel of SA, there’s an SA or DSA-style set that will make your Planck or Preonic look and feel the way you want. Pick a profile (uniform DSA vs. sculpted SA), then choose a colorway that fits your desk. Your ortho keyboard will thank you.
