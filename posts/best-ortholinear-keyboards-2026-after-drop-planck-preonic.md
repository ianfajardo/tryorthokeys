## What Actually Happened

In early 2026, Drop [announced its integration with CORSAIR](/posts/drop-ecommerce-store-closing-march-31-keyboard-club) and shut down its standalone ecommerce store as of March 31st. The Drop Keyboard Club ended at the same time. Drop had been the primary retail channel for the OLKB Planck and Preonic for years, so when the store went dark, two of the most recognizable ortholinear keyboards effectively lost their main distribution path. Some Planck V7 stock still circulates on the secondhand market and a handful of Amazon listings, but for most buyers in 2026, the answer to "where do I buy a Planck?" is "you probably don't."

The good news: the ortholinear category is healthier than it has been in years. There are more 40% and 50% boards available, more wireless options, more low-profile builds, and more keycap support than there was at the Planck's peak. Here's the short list worth your attention.

Drop was also the easiest place to grab MT3 ortho sets. With Drop closed, here's where to look:

- **[FKcaps](/keycaps#FKcaps)** — fully customizable Cherry/DSA/MDA/Choc sets. We have a 10% off referral link on the [keycaps page](/keycaps).
- **[Chosfox](/keycaps#chosfox)** — low-profile and Choc-compatible sets that pair perfectly with the Geonix, Israfel, or any low-profile ortho.

- **[Pimp My Keyboard](/keycaps#pimpmykeyboard)** — SA, DSA, and DCS sets, many with ortho add-on kits.
- **Amazon** — MT3 sets that previously sold on Drop (BoW, WoB, Dasher, Cyber, /dev/tty, Susuwatari) are still listed via Amazon. See our [Drop keycaps section](/keycaps#drop) for the current Amazon links.

For a deeper dive specifically on Planck/Preonic-compatible caps, our older guide on [the best keycaps on Drop for Planck and Preonic](/posts/best-keycaps-on-drop-for-planck-preonic) still has useful profile information even though the Drop links themselves are dead.

## Should You Still Buy a Planck Secondhand?

If you find a Planck V6 or V7 secondhand at a reasonable price and you specifically want the original, sure — it's a great keyboard and the community will keep supporting QMK on it for years. But for someone choosing a new board in 2026, the **Geonix Rev.2** matches or beats the Planck on every spec sheet line (wireless, low-profile, aluminum, hot-swap, QMK/VIA) for the same money. The romantic answer is "buy a Planck." The practical answer is "buy the Geonix."
